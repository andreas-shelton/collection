Every Piece of Equipment I Use to Listen to Music

My phone that comes with me everywhere and is mainly used in combination with spotify.

A recent acquisition. My Ipod Classic 7th gen that I use as an mp3 player in tandem with a number of other methods to put said files onto it.

My computer that I use for just about everything. In this context, it serves both as a player for apps like spotify, as a storage device for the files that go onto the Ipod, and as a way to source and convert new music into files that can be read by my other devices.

My many earbuds that one or multiple come with me everywhere. Each serves its own purpose, and they are vital to my mobile listening experience.

My headphones which mainly see use at home but are also mobile if neccesary. Can be used to connect to any of the previous devices via bluetooth. Offer the best sound quality of the mobile listening tools but at the expense of practicality.

One fourth of my at home physical media listening setup. A technics sl-220 record player I thrifted some time ago.

The second fourth of my at home physical media listening setup. A sony 5 disc cd player that I bought from a local music store a little bit ago.

The third part to my at home physical media listening setup. The Denon PMA-600NE amplifier that makes it all work.

The fourth and final part of my at home physical media listening setup. Two speakers gifted from my father.

Other essential parts not pictured above: the many cords that make it all run, the many cds and vinyl that are used with the system, and the cd reader that I use to pull music from the cds to my computer. Physical media might just be my biggest love, and one of the worst things for my bank account. I like to try and make it my whole personality to make it worth it.
